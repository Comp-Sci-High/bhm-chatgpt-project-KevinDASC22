The NBA's Stand for Social Justice

How the league took action during the 2020 season and its impact on the BLM movement.

What Did the NBA Do?

During the 2020 season, the NBA made several commitments to racial justice.

The decision to paint *Black Lives Matter* on all courts during the 2020 season was a significant statement. It showed solidarity with the movement and ensured that the message was seen by millions of viewers worldwide. The visibility helped spark conversations and reinforced the NBA‚Äôs commitment to social justice.

Players were allowed to replace their last names with social justice messages like *Equality*, *Say Their Names*, and *I Can't Breathe*. This was a powerful way for players to use their platform to spread awareness, and it encouraged fans to learn more about these issues.

The Milwaukee Bucks led a historic boycott by refusing to play in response to the police shooting of Jacob Blake. This led to a temporary suspension of NBA games and forced national conversations about racial injustice, police reform, and the role of athletes in activism.

The NBA and its players committed to creating a $300 million foundation aimed at supporting economic empowerment in Black communities. This fund helps create job opportunities, educational programs, and financial support for Black-owned businesses.
